Ten centuries ago,<br />before the Age of Obedience,
free dogs roamed at liberty,<br />marking their territory.
Seeking to extend<br />its dominion,
the cat-loving<br />Kobayashi Dynasty declared war
and descended in force
upon the unwary<br />four-legged beasts.
On the eve<br />of total canine annihilation,
a child warrior<br />sympathetic to the plight
of the besieged underdog dogs
betrayed his species,
beheaded the head of the head<br />of the Kobayashi clan
and pledged his sword
with the following<br />battle-cry haiku.
He would later be known
as the Boy Samurai<br />of Legend, RIP.
At the end<br />of the bloody dog wars,
the vanquished mongrels<br />became powerless house-pets:
tamed, mastered, scorned.
But they survived<br />and multiplied.
The Kobayashis, however,
never forgave<br />their conquered foe.
