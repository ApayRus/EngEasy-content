# 39. Modal Verbs (can, must, should, may)

## New words {new}

can
can't
could
couldn't
must
mustn't
should
shouldn't
may
might

## Can / Can't (Ability)

I can speak English.
You can run fast.
He can cook dinner.
She can dance well.
It can run.
We can swim in the river.
They can play well.
I can't hear you.
You can't see me.
He can't buy a car.
She can't find her keys.
We can't sleep now.
They can't understand us.
Can you help me?
Can I open the window?
Can he read this book?
Can we go home?
I can wait for you.
She can't come today.
We can buy this house.

## Must / Mustn't (Obligation)

I must go now.
You must work hard.
He must sleep.
She must call her mother.
We must buy food.
They must study English.
I must clean my room.
You must listen to me.
He must drink water.
She must wear clean clothes.
You mustn't sleep here.
He mustn't speak loud.
She mustn't be late.
We mustn't close this.
They mustn't open the door.
You mustn't eat in the library.
I must finish this work.
He must help his father.
We must wake up early.
You mustn't run in school.

## Should / Shouldn't (Advice)

You should sleep more.
He should eat good food.
She should buy a new car.
We should go to the doctor.
They should read this book.
I should call him.
You should study today.
He should change clothes.
She should drink hot tea.
We should clean the house.
You shouldn't eat bad food.
He shouldn't drink cold water.
She shouldn't watch TV at night.
We shouldn't be late.
They shouldn't speak now.
You shouldn't buy this phone.
I shouldn't do that.
He shouldn't go there.
We should help them.
You shouldn't cry.

## Could / Couldn't (Past Ability / Politeness)

I could swim when I was five.
He could read a long book.
She could dance well.
We could see the mountains.
They could hear the song.
I couldn't sleep yesterday.
He couldn't find his bag.
She couldn't cook dinner.
We couldn't open the window.
They couldn't answer the question.
Could you help me?
Could you give me water?
Could we go now?
Could I take this?
Could he speak English?

## May / Might (Possibility)

It may be cold today.
He may come at night.
She may be at home.
We may go to the cinema.
They may buy a dog.
I might be late.
You might like this movie.
He might know the answer.
She might call you.
We might see him there.
May I come in?
May I ask a question?
May I sit here?
It might be cold outside.
They might not know.
